# Opinionated Imagen — Nail Content Ekip

Salon photos, made postable. Not a tool. A personal content ekip that turns everyday nail work into polished posts.

For nail salons and independent nail techs, this means no more posting dim chair photos, awkward hand crops, messy station backgrounds, or mismatched carousels because there is no content person on staff. They upload real nail photos, teach the system their salon style, and get back content that looks edited by someone who understands beauty-service marketing.

The salon uploads its work photos. The system learns the salon's visual standard: nail finish, hand posing, lighting, backdrop taste, and posting style. The salon picks a **Scene** such as a client-result close-up or before/after carousel. The system answers back: "This is what I understood." They check it, tweak it once if it is wrong, and hit process. **The Edit** comes back: a curated sheet of post-ready variations. No prompt engineering. No fake beauty shoot. One turn, one **Brief**, one **Drop**.

## Why This Niche

PhotoAI ships 30+ features. Most salons need three outcomes: make the result photo cleaner, make the hands and nails look intentional, and make the carousel match the salon brand. The opportunity is in the opposite direction. Make it about one visual business done well. Make everyday phone photos look like a content person cleaned them up before posting.

The positioning: this is not an AI product. It is a premium content service for nail businesses. "Processing your Drop" not "Generating." "The Brief" not "Prompt Preview." "Scene" not "Preset." "The Edit" not "AI Output."

## Who

**Maya.** Independent nail tech or small salon owner. Posts client work to Instagram weekly because bookings depend on trust, taste, and recency. Her work is good, but her phone photos often have harsh salon lighting, crowded backgrounds, awkward hand angles, and inconsistent color. She cares about quality enough to pay for it, but not enough to learn Lightroom, prompts, or content systems.

She is not a prompt engineer or a photo editor.

## Core Flow

### 1. Onboard — Step 1: Work Source

Maya uploads a **Work Set** — real photos of nails she has done. At least 10. These do not need to be perfect; they need to show nail shape, finish, skin tone handling, hand positioning, and common salon lighting.

Behind the scenes: the system starts extracting the product-critical visual truth: nail finish, hand anatomy, color fidelity, and the salon's baseline photo problems. The client and nail result must stay believable.

### 2. Onboard — Step 2: Salon Style

Maya picks a **Vibe** presented as visual cards: clean luxury, soft pink studio, glossy editorial, warm neighborhood salon, minimal product detail. She picks the one that matches the salon brand.

Alternative: if she already has a strong feed, she taps "Upload my style" and uploads salon posts she likes. The system extracts the salon aesthetic automatically.

Result: one consistent style is locked for the salon's output.

### 3. Create

She arrives at her dashboard. Profile is built. She browses **Scenes** or types freeform. "Client Result Close-Up." "Before/After Set." "Salon Station Carousel." "Make this chrome set look clean and premium."

### 4. The Brief

The system answers with a plain-language paragraph: "Chrome almond set on natural hands, clean salon lighting, soft pink background. 8 shots: 3 close-ups, 2 hand poses, 2 detail crops, 1 story frame."

Every clause is editable. She tweaks once, or not at all. Then she hits Process. No preview image. Just confirmation of what the system understood.

### 5. Process

The system generates against the confirmed Brief. The Edit comes back in a few minutes.

### 6. Archive

She sees The Edit. She saves shots to her Archive. She discards what she doesn't want. She can remix a saved shot into a story crop, carousel cover, or before/after Drop.
